Udaipur Destination Wedding Photographer: Palaces, Costs & Tips (2026)

By WeddingClickz•Updated 10 May 2026•10 min read

Udaipur is the uncontested capital of destination weddings in India. Lake Pichola, the Aravalli hills, medieval palace courtyards and centuries of Rajput grandeur create a backdrop that no studio can replicate and no other city in India can match. It is also — for exactly these reasons — one of the most photographically demanding wedding locations in the country.

This guide is for couples planning a Udaipur wedding who want to understand: which venues photograph best, what it actually costs to bring a professional photography team, how to navigate drone permits, and how to book before the dates are gone.

Why Udaipur Is India's #1 Destination Wedding Location

Udaipur has three things no other Indian city combines at the same scale:

  • Water + architecture — Lake Pichola with the City Palace rising behind it is the most photographed wedding backdrop in Asia. The boat arrival and mandap-on-the-lake shots are impossible anywhere else.
  • Golden hour light — The Aravalli terrain creates a warm, directional sunset that turns everything ochre and gold from 5pm to 6:30pm. Photographers call this the "Udaipur hour" — it is the most forgiving and most beautiful natural light in India for portraiture.
  • Heritage density — Within a 20km radius: lake palaces, hilltop forts, haveli courtyards, temple ghats and Rajput banquet halls. A 3-day wedding can shoot a different architecturally distinct location every single event.

The Top Udaipur Wedding Venues — Photographically Ranked

1. Taj Lake Palace

The most iconic wedding venue in India. The hotel sits in the middle of Lake Pichola with the City Palace and Aravalli hills as the backdrop. Drone flight is heavily restricted due to proximity to the airport and controlled airspace — the real aerial shots require advance DGCA clearance and are not guaranteed. The hero shots here are the boat arrival, the lake-facing mandap at sunset, and the rooftop portraits.

Photography note: The white marble interior creates harsh reflected light during midday. Schedule indoor ceremony photography in the early morning (7–9am) or late afternoon (4–6pm) if possible.

2. RAAS Devigarh

A restored 18th-century Rajput fort 35km north of Udaipur on a hilltop. The most consistently drone-permitting luxury venue near Udaipur — the elevated topography and distance from the airport makes DGCA clearance more achievable. The exterior fort walls, the infinity pool, and the terrace at sunset produce the most dramatic aerial wedding photographs in Rajasthan. Intimate (max 200 guests) and cooler than central Udaipur.

3. The Leela Palace Udaipur

On the banks of Lake Pichola with a lakeview mandap that mirrors the Taj's setup at a somewhat lower price point. The Leela has more flexible outdoor space and has historically permitted drone operations with advance venue coordination. The ballroom has a Rajasthani-themed interior with excellent natural light from tall windows.

4. Shiv Niwas Palace

Part of the City Palace complex, with access to the palace courtyard and the famous sweeping lake views. The mix of Rajput and colonial architecture creates natural photography frames at every corner. Indoor wedding photography in the heritage rooms — with their carved marble pillars and antique chandeliers — is exceptional.

5. Amet Haveli

The most intimate and romantic option — a heritage lakeside haveli with a direct Lake Pichola frontage. Works best for weddings under 100 guests. The small terrace at sunset with the lake behind is a photographer's dream for couple portraiture. No drone space, but the ground-level lakeside photography compensates fully.

6. Fateh Garh

A converted 18th-century fort with commanding views of the Aravalli hills and Udaipur city below. The fort walls and tower provide natural elevated vantage points for dramatic wide shots. Drone operations have historically been permitted here with advance coordination. The outdoor ceremony space against the fort wall is architecturally unmatched.

Drone Photography at Udaipur: The Reality

Udaipur falls in a complex airspace zone. Maharana Pratap Airport creates a controlled zone that covers most of the city centre, including Lake Pichola and the City Palace area. Here is what this means practically:

  • Taj Lake Palace, Shiv Niwas, Amet Haveli — drone flights are very difficult to permit and often denied
  • RAAS Devigarh and Fateh Garh — permits are achievable with 3–4 weeks advance DGCA application
  • Farmhouses and resorts 15+ km from the city — generally easier to permit

The solution for city-centre venues: engage a drone operator who applies through the Digital Sky platform at least 30 days in advance, files for a specific time window, and has a backup plan if clearance is denied on the day.

The Ideal Photography Timeline for a 3-Day Udaipur Wedding

  • Day 1 – Mehendi / Sangeet: Late afternoon into evening, typically at the resort pool or garden. Golden hour starts at 5:30pm — plan at least one couple portrait session at this time, even 15 minutes.
  • Day 2 – Wedding Ceremony: Morning muhurtham (7–11am) or evening ceremony (5–8pm). Evening ceremonies at Udaipur venues against the lake and setting sun are among the most photographed Indian wedding frames in the world.
  • Day 3 – Cocktail / Reception: Usually indoor or poolside. The City Palace lights reflecting in the lake at night provide a dramatic reception backdrop if your venue has a water view.

How to Book a Photographer for Your Udaipur Wedding

  1. Start 12–18 months ahead for December–February dates. Peak season Udaipur bookings from premium photographers are gone by January of the preceding year.
  2. Ask for a Udaipur-specific portfolio — not just destination weddings in general. Light conditions, architectural types and logistical demands are specific. Confirm they have shot at your exact venue or a comparable one.
  3. Clarify who handles drone permits — you or the photographer? At WeddingClickz, we handle all DGCA paperwork as part of the destination package.
  4. Confirm venue photography policies — some Udaipur heritage properties restrict flash, require photography timing to end before a certain hour, or charge a venue fee for external production teams. Know this before you book both the venue and the photographer.
  5. Factor in a venue recce trip — for weddings over ₹50L budget, request that the lead photographer visits the venue 1–2 days before the event to pre-plan all camera positions, lighting, and drone flight paths.

Which Udaipur venue is best for wedding photography?

RAAS Devigarh for drone aerial photography and hillside grandeur. Taj Lake Palace for the lake + palace backdrop and boat arrival shots. Fateh Garh for fort architecture and drone-permissible hilltop aerials. Amet Haveli for intimate lakeside portraiture. All four consistently produce world-class wedding photography.

Is drone photography allowed at Udaipur palace weddings?

City-centre venues (Taj Lake Palace, Shiv Niwas) are in restricted airspace and drone permits are difficult to obtain. RAAS Devigarh and Fateh Garh are more achievable with 30-day advance DGCA applications. Begin the permit process no later than 4 weeks before the wedding date.

How far in advance should I book a Udaipur wedding photographer?

12–18 months for October–February peak season dates. Premium photographers with Udaipur palace portfolios are booked 18 months ahead for marquee weekends. If your date is 6 months away, contact studios immediately to check availability.
