Pre-Wedding Shoot Ideas India 2025: Locations, Outfits & Cost Guide

By WeddingClickz•Updated 10 May 2026•11 min read

A pre-wedding shoot is no longer optional for most Indian couples — it is the social media announcement, the save-the-date, the Instagram grid moment, and the chance to get comfortable in front of the camera before the wedding day. Done well, it is also the most relaxed photography you will ever do as a couple.

Why Do a Pre-Wedding Shoot?

  • Camera comfort — practice being in front of the lens before the real wedding day so you are relaxed, not stiff
  • Photographer chemistry — build rapport and communication with your photographer well in advance
  • Content — create images for wedding invites, save-the-dates, WhatsApp announcements and Instagram
  • Your story — capture your relationship in a setting you choose, not the mandap you are assigned

Best Pre-Wedding Shoot Locations by City

Bangalore

  • Cubbon Park — colonial architecture, dappled tree light, urban greenery. Best at 6:30–8am before crowds arrive.
  • Lalbagh Botanical Garden — glass house, lake reflections, 200-year-old trees. Golden hour at the rose garden.
  • Nandi Hills — sunrise fog and dramatic valley landscapes 2 hours from the city. Ideal for adventure-oriented couples.
  • Palace Grounds — heritage Indo-Saracenic arches and manicured lawns. Permission required.
  • Coorg — coffee estates, misty hills, waterfalls and wooden homestays. 3-hour drive; overnight recommended.

Mumbai

  • Bandra Bandstand & Worli Sea Face — urban waterfront, twilight Mumbai skyline, industrial-chic aesthetic
  • Kanheri Caves, Borivali — 2nd-century rock-cut Buddhist architecture, jungle canopy
  • Alibaug — coastal, rustic, European colonial forts, 1.5-hour ferry across the bay
  • Palghar Flower Fields (seasonal) — yellow mustard and sunflower fields in January–February

Udaipur & Rajasthan

  • City Palace terraces — Lake Pichola backdrop, the most photographed palace setting in India
  • Jagmandir Island — boat ride out, royal archways over the lake, exclusive access
  • Nahargarh Fort, Jaipur — panoramic Pink City view, blue architecture of the old city
  • Sam Sand Dunes, Jaisalmer — golden hour magic with camel silhouettes
  • Stepwells (Chand Baori, Abhaneri) — geometric stone architecture, completely unique worldwide

Goa

  • Fontainhas Latin Quarter — Portuguese heritage lanes, coloured tiled facades, street art
  • Basilica of Bom Jesus complex — 400-year-old architecture, dramatic stone archways
  • Chapora Fort — cliffside ocean panorama, dramatic sunset silhouettes
  • Agonda or Palolem Beach — quiet, beautiful, fewer crowds than North Goa

Kerala

  • Alleppey Backwaters — houseboat shoot at dawn, water reflections, coconut palms
  • Munnar Tea Estates — misty, emerald green terraced hillsides
  • Fort Kochi — Chinese fishing nets, Dutch cemetery, colonial heritage lanes

Golden rule: Complement, do not match. Coordinate on a colour family, not identical outfits. Avoid busy prints that compete with the background. Solid colours and subtle textures photograph best.

Timing: The Secret to Beautiful Light

Golden hour — 45 minutes after sunrise or 60 minutes before sunset — gives warm, directional light that flatters all skin tones and creates the long soft shadows you see in every dream pre-wedding image. This is not optional; it is the difference between ordinary and extraordinary results.

Avoid shooting between 11am and 3pm in outdoor locations. Harsh overhead sun creates unflattering shadows, squinting, and blown-out skin tones. If you must shoot midday, choose shaded indoor locations or covered outdoor areas.

How to Brief Your Photographer

  1. Share 10–15 reference images from Pinterest or Instagram before the meeting
  2. Describe your vibe clearly: romantic and intimate, playful and fun, cinematic and dramatic, editorial and fashion-forward
  3. Walk the primary location with your photographer before shoot day if possible
  4. Plan 2–3 outfit changes for variety in the final gallery
  5. Tell your photographer about 2–3 things that define your relationship — these often become the most authentic moments
Is a pre-wedding shoot necessary?

Not strictly necessary, but strongly recommended for two reasons: (1) it gets you comfortable in front of the camera before your actual wedding day, and (2) it gives you high-quality content for invitations, social announcements and wedding decor. Couples who do a pre-wedding shoot are visibly more relaxed in their wedding-day photographs.

Can we do a pre-wedding shoot close to the wedding date?

Yes, but 2–3 months before the wedding is ideal. Closer to the date, logistical pressures (venue visits, vendor meetings, family obligations) make it harder to be relaxed. You also want editing turnaround time before you need to use the images for invitations.
