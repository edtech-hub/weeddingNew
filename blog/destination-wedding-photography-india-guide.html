Destination Wedding Photography in India: Udaipur, Goa, Jaipur & Kerala

By WeddingClickz•Updated 10 May 2026•12 min read

India is one of the world's most spectacular destinations for weddings — palace courtyards, backwater sunsets, sand dune horizons, and tropical coastlines that no studio set can replicate. Capturing this properly requires a photographer who has worked in these environments before, understands the logistics, and can move with a large wedding party across multiple venues in a single day.

Top Destination Wedding Locations in India

Udaipur, Rajasthan — The City of Lakes

Udaipur is India's most photographed wedding destination. The combination of Lake Pichola reflections, City Palace architecture, and Aravalli range sunsets creates cinematic backdrops at every turn. Almost every shot from a Udaipur wedding is publication-worthy without significant effort — which is why it commands premium pricing.

Key venues: Taj Lake Palace (island hotel, no ground access), Leela Palace Udaipur, Devi Garh by Lebua (converted 18th-century fort), Oberoi Udaivilas (250 acres on the lake).

Drone note: Permits required. Apply through the venue 2 weeks minimum in advance.

Goa — Three Visual Identities

Beach weddings, Portuguese heritage churches and colonial mansions give Goa three distinct visual identities in one state. November–February is peak for golden light and dry weather. Avoid May–September (monsoon) unless specifically shooting for dramatic rain aesthetics.

Key venues: Taj Exotica Resort & Spa, W Goa, Alila Diwa, Casa de Campo (private villa rentals in North Goa), Kenilworth Beach Resort.

Jaipur, Rajasthan — The Pink City

Forts (Nahargarh, Amer), havelis, and block-print textiles create an unmistakably royal aesthetic. Jaipur is the capital of large Marwari wedding productions with elaborate multi-day events across multiple venues. The city's infrastructure for large weddings — decor vendors, caterers, entertainment — is unmatched outside Mumbai.

Key venues: Samode Palace, Rambagh Palace (former royal residence), Chomu Palace, Jai Mahal Palace.

Kerala — Cultural Depth

Backwater houseboats, Chinese fishing nets, paddy fields and temple architecture create an entirely different mood — lush, tranquil, and deeply rooted in culture. The Kerala Sadya banana-leaf feast setting alone produces photographs that are instantly recognisable and unlike anything from any other Indian state.

Key venues: Kumarakom Lake Resort, Marari Beach Resort, Brunton Boatyard (Fort Kochi), Coconut Lagoon by CGH Earth.

Mussoorie & Himachal Pradesh — Mountain Weddings

Pine forests, Himalayan panoramas and colonial-era heritage hotels are rapidly growing as a category. The dramatic elevation change and crystalline Himalayan light create extraordinary landscape photography that is impossible to replicate anywhere else in India.

Key venues: Wildflower Hall Shimla (former home of Lord Kitchener), Welcomhotel Elysium Shimla, Sterling Mussoorie.

Destination Wedding Photography Logistics Checklist

  • Book your photographer 10–14 months ahead for peak-season (Oct–Feb) Rajasthan and Goa dates
  • Share venue floor plans, multi-day event schedule and vendor contact list 4 weeks before
  • Confirm drone no-fly zones with the venue and local authorities at least 3 weeks before
  • Arrange a venue recce walk with your lead photographer the morning before Day 1
  • Designate a family coordinator to gather relatives for scheduled group shots
  • Confirm all travel logistics in writing — flights, accommodation, local transport

How to Choose Your Destination Wedding Photographer

  • Location experience — ask specifically if they have shot at your destination before, and request a full gallery from that location
  • Multi-day stamina — review galleries from 2- and 3-day weddings, not just single-day events
  • Transparent travel pricing — all travel costs should be itemised in the contract before signing
  • Backup equipment policy — destination shoots have zero margin for equipment failure; dual-slot cameras and backup lenses are non-negotiable

Should I hire a local photographer or bring my own for a destination wedding?

Bringing your own photographer — one whose work you have already vetted with full galleries — is almost always the better choice. Local photographers at destination venues are often good at standard shots but may not understand your specific cultural rituals or match your aesthetic expectations. The travel cost addition is typically 10–20% of the photography package, which is worth the certainty.

What is the best season for a Rajasthan destination wedding?

October to February is peak season in Rajasthan — temperatures are 15–25°C, skies are clear blue, and golden hour light is exceptional. March–April is increasingly popular as an off-peak alternative with fewer crowds and comparable light. Avoid May–September (extreme heat, 40°C+).
